Global University Prestige Ranking via Faculty PhD-to-Employment Flow Networks

💡 What is XRank?

Traditional university rankings rely heavily on subjective surveys, financial resources, or research outputs. XRank offers a mathematically rigorous, globally fair alternative by mapping the PhD-to-Faculty employment flow network. Using a consolidated dataset across Wikidata, OpenAlex, and ORCID APIs, XRank runs a Weighted PageRank algorithm to compute the absolute prestige of universities based on where their PhD graduates go to teach.

📖 Metric Explanations

XRank Score

The consolidated academic prestige value calculated via Weighted PageRank (multiplied by 1,000 for legibility). It represents the probability of reaching a university by tracing academic career trajectories. The higher the score, the more dominant a university is as a producer of prestigious faculty members.

Total Placed

The total count of PhD graduates from a given university who are currently employed as faculty members across all QS Top 100 institutions in our dataset. In network graph theory, this corresponds to the university's out-degree weight in the hiring network.

Δ (Prestige Shift)

The change in position when comparing the university's XRank (academic placement prestige) against its official **QS World Ranking**. A positive green shift ($\Delta$) indicates that a school's actual academic placement power is stronger than its general QS reputation suggests.
