💡 What is FairRank?

Traditional university rankings rely heavily on subjective surveys, financial resources, or research outputs. FairRank maps the PhD-to-faculty employment flow network to estimate academic placement prestige from open data. Using a consolidated dataset across Wikidata, OpenAlex, and ORCID APIs, FairRank runs a Weighted PageRank algorithm to score universities based on where their PhD graduates go on to teach. The result is an open placement-prestige signal, not a complete measure of university quality.

📖 Metric Explanations

FairRank Score

The academic placement-prestige value calculated via Weighted PageRank (multiplied by 1,000 for legibility). It represents the probability of reaching a university by tracing observed faculty hiring paths. Higher scores indicate stronger observed placement into influential academic employers.

External Placed

The count of observed PhD graduates from a given university who are employed as faculty at other institutions in the dataset. Self-hires are excluded to match the PageRank graph. With edges directed from hiring institution to alma mater, this is the university's weighted incoming placement count.

Δ (Prestige Shift)

The change in position when comparing the university's FairRank placement-prestige rank against its official QS World Ranking. A positive green shift indicates that a school's observed academic placement signal is stronger than its general QS position suggests.

Hiring Connections

The total number of active directed employment edges in the academic network graph after self-loops are removed. Each connection represents a pathway where an alumnus of an alma mater is employed as faculty at a hiring university.

Damping Factor (0.85)

A standard parameter in the PageRank algorithm. It means 85% of score propagation follows observed placement links, while 15% is redistributed across the graph to ensure convergence and stability.

Data Source: OpenAlex + ORCID + Wikidata · Algorithm: Weighted PageRank (d=0.85) · Self-loops removed
